FOV = Field Of View. It is how wide the player can see — like human peripheral vision. A narrow FOV feels zoomed in (like binoculars). A wide FOV feels wide but distorts the edges.
66°
Standard cub3D FOV = 66°. This comes from the camera plane length = 0.66.
Formula: FOV = 2 * atan(plane_length) * (180/PI)
So: 2 * atan(0.66) * 57.3 ≈ 66°
The camera plane is a line perpendicular to the direction the player faces. Its width controls FOV. Think of it as the "screen" in the 2D world that the player projects onto.
0°
Rule: dir and plane must ALWAYS be perpendicular. When dir rotates 30°, plane also rotates 30°. They are always 90° apart. If you break this rule, your FOV distorts.
For each screen column (0 to WIDTH-1), you shoot one ray. The ray direction is computed from dir + plane * cameraX.
cameraX = 2*x/WIDTH - 1 → goes from -1 (left edge) to +1 (right edge).
160
cameraX = 2 * x / 320 - 1
ray_dir_x = dir_x + plane_x * cameraX
ray_dir_y = dir_y + plane_y * cameraX

The highlighted ray is the one for the selected column. All other rays shown in gray.
Drag the slider to rotate the player. Watch how dir, plane, and all rays rotate together as one unit.
